Prompt: What does the sentence 'The chicken is ready to eat' mean?
Response:
 The sentence "The chicken is ready to eat" implies that the chicken, which was previously raw or uncooked, has been fully cooked and is now suitable for consumption. However, it doesn't specify whether the chicken is still whole or if it has been cut into portions. It also doesn't state who should consume the chicken, but typically it suggests that the person preparing the meal has completed their task.
